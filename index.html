The fall and rise of drug crimes in San Fransisco

Introduction

The Fall

From 2003 to 2021, drug offenses in San Francisco fell sharply. In 2003, there were 7,468 recorded incidents. By 2021, that number had dropped to just 913. The decline was not smooth every single year, but the overall direction is unmistakable. The late 2000s marked the high point, with 8,405 incidents in 2008 and 7,807 in 2009. After that, the numbers fell fast: 5,871 in 2010, 4,365 in 2011, 3,424 in 2014, 2,047 in 2017, and under 1,000 by 2021. Looked at broadly, this is a story of steady collapse.

What makes the pattern more striking is where the incidents were concentrated. The Tenderloin was already the leading district in 2003, with 2,038 incidents, or 27.3% of the city total. At that point, it was still part of a broader cluster. Mission accounted for 21.3% and Southern for 21.1%, so drug offenses were heavily concentrated in a few districts rather than spread evenly across the city. But over time, the Tenderloin pulled further ahead.

By 2005, the Tenderloin accounted for 35.0% of all incidents. In 2008, it reached 40.8%, meaning roughly two out of every five drug offense incidents in San Francisco were happening there. Even as the citywide total began to fall after 2009, the Tenderloin remained the dominant center. In 2010 it still represented 31.2% of all incidents. In 2014, despite the much lower citywide total, it remained the top district. In 2015, it still held first place.

There was a brief interruption in 2016 and 2017, when Southern narrowly overtook the Tenderloin. But that shift did not last. By 2018, the Tenderloin was back on top with 35.8% of all incidents. In 2019, it held 33.7%. Then the concentration became even stronger. In 2020, the Tenderloin accounted for 51.2% of all recorded drug offenses in San Francisco. In 2021, it rose to 55.4%. At that point, more than half of the city's drug offense incidents were tied to a single district.

That is the real shape of the story. Drug offenses did not simply decline across San Francisco in a uniform way. They shrank across the city while becoming more concentrated in the Tenderloin. As the overall numbers fell, the Tenderloin became more central, not less. The citywide decline and the Tenderloin's growing dominance happened at the same time. That is what gives the period its character: fewer incidents overall, but an ever larger share centered in one neighborhood.

The Rise

Around 2022 drug crimes started to increase dramatically, and the ratio of drug crimes compared to other crimes also increased, shooting all the way up to 23% of all crimes in November 2025, from the low of 2.5% in 2021. There are a number of ways to interpret the reason for this sharp increase during the last couple of years, and multiple factors likely contribute to the reason, with some of the reasons including fentanyl and more crackdown on drugs.

Fentanyl, among other synthetic opioids have gained much ground since 2019 and are significantly more cheap in San Fransisco than other places in the United States such as New York. [1]

From 2018 until 2020 drug overdose deaths in San Fransisco almost tripled going from 259 to 726 deaths, with most of those deaths being attributed to fentanyl. [2]

The response to this drug crisis has been more crackdown on drugs and in november 2023 the All Hands on Deck initiative was announced which brought together local, state and federal agents to tackle the drug epidemic in San Fransisco. [3]
